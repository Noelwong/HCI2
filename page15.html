Associate of Science
in Environmental Studies

  • Career Prospects
  • Main Subject Areas
  • General Education Courses
  • Mode of Study

Explain concepts and methods fundamental to environmental studies;
Analyse and interpret data using quantitative methods, and information technology systems, particularly with regard to environmental data, and especially for the purpose of planning and management;
Relate environmental issues, activities, planning and management practices, as appropriate, to the socio-political, cultural and economic contexts of the region and beyond, as a participating and responsible member of civic society;
Perform skilled tasks responsibly, in accordance with ethical norms, and produce outputs that meet quality standards;
Communicate effectively, both orally and in writing, on environmental issues using English;
Relate the major ideas in arts and humanities, science and technology to their own area of study.

Graduates in this programme will be eligible for a broad range of jobs career paths leading to opportunities such as Environmental Technician, Assistant Environmental Scientist, Environmental Officer, Environmental Protection Inspector in the Environmental Protection Department; Health Inspector in the Food and Environmental Hygiene Department; Country Park Officer in the Fisheries and Conservation Department; Marketing Executives in occupational health and environmental safety products; Environmental Planner in property development companies, or architectural firms, etc..
